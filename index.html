Poverty and Corruption

Community Voices break the Cycle

Corruption perpetuates poverty. It skews decisions and diverts scarce funds, denying poor people access to basic social services and resources to improve their livelihoods. Where better-off people can find private solutions to public failures - water filtration, generators, private schools and hospitals - disadvantaged groups have no alternative. For them, corruption creates a vicious cycle. If poor people can't speak up for their rights and demand accountability from their leaders, their needs aren't met and they can't escape poverty
